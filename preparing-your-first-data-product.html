Preparing Your First Data Product

A practical guide to scoping, modelling, governing and publishing a data product — written for teams who have never shipped one.

📘 5-8 page PDF · ~10 minute read

Why most data products fail before they start

The problem is rarely technical. Most data products fail because they were built without a clear consumer, a defined scope, or an owner who cares about them beyond the initial project. This guide walks you through the five steps that prevent those failures.

"If you can't describe who the consumer is and what question the data answers, you don't have a data product — you have a table."

What's inside

  • Step 1: Start with the question, not the data — how to validate demand before you write a single line of code
  • Step 2: Define scope and grain — the single most important design decision, and how to get it right
  • Step 3: Assign ownership and governance — why "the data team" is not an owner
  • Step 4: Build, test, and document — the implementation pattern that works across platforms
  • Step 5: Publish and iterate — ship the 80%, get feedback, improve

Plus: a readiness checklist

A one-page checklist covering scope, ownership, build quality, metadata, and launch readiness. Use it as a gate before publishing any data product.

Who it's for

Data leaders, product owners, architects, and analysts building their first data product — on any platform. The principles are platform-agnostic; the examples lean toward SAP Datasphere because that's where the author has spent the most time.
